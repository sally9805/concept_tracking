This enterprise , considered as a private undertaking , was equally marked with sagacity and commercial courage . In its connexion with public interests , it bid fair to be of vast political importance . " A cache is a term common among traders and hunters , to designate a hiding-place for provisions and effects . It is derived from the French word cacher , to conceal , ' and originated among the early colonists of Canada and Louisiana ; but the secret depository which it designates was in use among the aboriginals long before the intrusion of the white men . It is , in fact , the only mode that migratory hordes have of preserving their valuables from robbery , during their long absences from their villages or accustomed haunts , on hunting expeditions , or during tle vicissitudes of war . The utmost skill and caution are required to render these places of concealment invisible to the lynx eye of an Indian . The first care is to seek out a proper situation , which is generally some dry low bank of clay , on the margin of a water-course . As soon as the precise spot is pitched upon , blankets , saddle-cloths , and other coverings are spread @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ , or any other derangement ; and as few hands as possible are employed .
