Since its yeasty youth under moon-shooting Idea Men Paul Hoffman and Robert Maynard Hutchins , the vast Ford Foundation ( $2.7 billion in current assets ) has grown more staid . Latest evidence : the appointment of Manhattan Banker John J. McCloy , U.S. High Commissioner for Germany from 1949 to 1952 , to succeed H. Rowan Gaither Jr . as board chairman . McCloy , 63 , will take over in December without leaving his post as board chairman of the Chase Manhattan <P> Bank . Gaither , who helped give the foundation its present shape and direction , announced last May that he would return to his San Francisco law practice . Still in office as the foundation 's guiding force and chief executive : able President Henry Heald.
