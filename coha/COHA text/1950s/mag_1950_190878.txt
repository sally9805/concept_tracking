In the midst of the hubbub over the Bell report , President Quirino announced that there was no national emergency in the troubled Philippines . Three days later , he suspended the writ of habeas corpus . His explanation : there was a national emergency . Said Quirino : " There is no doubt that the lawless elements have constituted their various groups into an organized effort to subvert the government . . . Numerous cases . . . have convinced me that there is an actual state of rebellion . . . there is danger this movement may extend throughout the country . " <P> Many Filipino politicians and newspapers promptly called Quirino 's action unconstitutional and unnecessary . They regarded it as evidence that his government was still not up to dealing effectively with the Communist-led Huk guerrillas .
