Do U.S. physicians hand out too many barbiturates ? Doctors prescribe from 3 Dillon to 4 billion doses each year and there are estimated to be at least 50,000 confirmed addicts besides a host of habitual users . Two Washington , B.C. researchers polled colleagues , reported in Postgraduate Medicine : 1 ) most uses of barbiturates are necessary or at least legitimate ; 2 ) unjustified prescriptions ( tor routine sedation or mild insomnia ) do not occur often enough to justify new control legislation ; 3 ) most doctors are eager to get rid of barbiturates , " are waiting only for the advancement of medical knowledge and the growth of psychiatric facilities " to cut down .
