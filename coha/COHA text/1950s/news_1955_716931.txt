txt The faint fragrance of carnations wafted over the Paris subway line between the Bastille and the Arc de Triomphe stations today . Officials ' took note as passengers ' expressions denoted either acceptance or disapproval . The underground had changed perfume for the third time in three months . The artificial aroma is intended ? pending better ventilating equipment  --  to overcome the combined odors of garlic , ripe cheese , tobacco smoke , and unwashed humans indigenous to the Paris tube . Dr. Bernard Lafay , health minister and head of the civic council , puts it politely when he said it was to make the Metro subway " more seductive . " On the line tested today , each train carried a giant spray which switched on automatically as the trains left each station , spraying the carnation essence in a thin cloud . Two perfumes tried previously proved inadequate .
