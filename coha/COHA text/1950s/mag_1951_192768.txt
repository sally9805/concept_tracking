The greatest strain on U.S.-British relations is the British belief that Americans do not understand that Britain would be wiped out in case of atomic war with Russia . In the Saturday Review of Literature , D. W. Brogan , an old hand at explaining Britons to Americans , and vice versa , puts the British concern this way : <P> " Some firm statements from Senators and U.S. publicists remind the British too much of the Irish landlord who wrote from London to his tenants in Connaught , ' If you think you can intimidate me by shooting my bailiff , you are much mistaken . ' The English are sometimes afraid they have been tapped for bailiff . "
