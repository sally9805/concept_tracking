In the Cairo Museum the small boy leaned over a case . The label said that it contained ten 14-lb. gold bars , dating from the Middle Kingdom and considered to be the world 's first gold money . As bored small boys will , he began to count . There were only eight . <P> His father summoned a guard . Two bars , worth an estimated $10,000 apiece melted down , had indeed disappeared . The case was opened . The remaining bars proved to be not gold but copper . <P> Some time , somehow , a highly ingenious thief had manufactured copper copies , embarrassed museum officials admitted last week , and substituted them for the gold originals , which presumably he sold for a tidy profit . And for months or years , the museum 's expert had been lecturing learnedly over the copper fakes without ever noticing the change .
