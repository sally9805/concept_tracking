Ten days after the first U.S. ground troops went into action in Korea June 25 , the U.N . Security Council asked 50 other U.N . member nations to supply troops . By last week , the replies added up to promises of about 27,000 fighting men . <P> The bulk of these would come from the British Commonwealth . Australia indicated that she would supply 10,000 men ; the United Kingdom at least a brigade ( about 5,000 men ) ; New Zealand a combat force of unspecified strength . ( At week 's end 4,000 New Zealanders had volunteered . ) Turkey offered 4,500 troops , Siam 4,000 ; Bolivia promised 30 officers .
