" In spite of our serious doubts , " wrote Soviet Premier Nikita Khrushchev to President Eisenhower last week , Russia is " prepared to try out " the Eisenhower proposal for joint technical studies on nuclear-test detection . Even with the " serious doubts " attached , this was a surprising concession ; Russia had rejected similar U.S. proposals time and again . <P> With the wariness of a man accepting a cigar from a notorious practical joker , the White House noted that the letter " seems " to accept the U.S. position on the need for joint technical studies as a possible " basis for progress toward agreement on disarmament . " Added Secretary of State Dulles , who has seen many a Red cigar explode : " We do n't get optimistic quickly about these matters , but at least there is some progress . "
