After a rash of scare stories in 1949 about bacteriological warfare , the late Defense Secretary James Forrestal issued a carefully worded official statement . The dangers of germs as a war weapon had been grossly exaggerated , he said , but " an active research program on biological war fare ... is being conducted in the interests of national defense . " Last week in Baltimore , Bacteriologist Perrin H. Long of Johns Hopkins Uni versity , addressing doctors interested in civil defense against atom bombs , called bacteriological warfare " bunk . " Scientific knowledge of the subject at the moment , he said , does not point to its use as a successful tactical weapon . Washington had no comment .
