It was a new-and absurd-low in Administration secrecy . The Congressional Joint Economic Committee planned to hold hearings last week on the state of the economy . Federal Reserve Chairman Arthur Burns and Council of Economic Advisers Chairman Herbert Stein readily agreed to give their assessments . But Kenneth Rush , Nixon 's new economic coordinator , refused on grounds of Executive privilege . <P> Having to testify , he said , would hinder his ability to give " candid and uninhibited " advice to Nixon in private . So the committee postponed the hearings , to the disappointment of members who had hoped to ask Rush what led the President to predict recently a late-1974 pickup in national production and a downturn in inflation .
