The federal bureaucracy has just finished saluting the industry that keeps it in business . President Nixon proclaimed last week the first National Microfilm Week in honor of the process that has made possible a vast accumulation of records for Social Security and more arcane institutions . It was an appropriate recognition , since the Federal Government is the nation 's most prodigious user of microfilm . Rivaling the marble palaces of Washington , D.C. , mountains of microfilm have been tirelessly assembled by diligent bureaucrats , who build with a blind devotion worthy of the men who once erected the Pyramids . Unfortunately , the monumental results of their labors are largely hidden from public view and sometimes labeled " Secret " or " Top secret " - unfit for most mortal eyes .
