txt MEMBERS OF THE Cook County College Teachers Union began voting Tuesday on the eight campuses of the City Colleges of Chicago on a proposal to go on strike Thursday over a contract dispute with the colleges ' board . The union broke off talks last Wednesday . On Sunday members voted to reject the board 's latest contract offer . A strike vote from the membership could disrupt fall registration this week and delay the opening of classes Monday for the system 's nearly 100,000 students . The union has demanded 10 per cent salary increases ; restoration of all budget cuts ; regular contracts for 100 teachers hired under five-month self-terminating contracts last April ; restoration of a discarded department system ; and job security and contracts for all teachers working in the colleges ' Chicago Skill Center . Balloting will continue Wednesday with an announcement of the outcome expected Wednesday night .
