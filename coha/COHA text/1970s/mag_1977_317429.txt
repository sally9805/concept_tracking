In their never-ending battle with coyotes , ranchers have used guns , traps and poison but still claim they lose $37 million worth of sheep each year to the predators . Now , after two years of experiments on 2,000 sheep , researchers at the University of Wyoming have come up with a pungent , Tabasco-like chemical compound synthesized from peppers that may be the most effective deterrent of all . When sprayed on sheep , the hot sauce chills a coyote 's appetite . One spraying can be effective for up to 28 days . But researchers admit to one obvious concern : coyotes might eventually learn to like , rather than shun , their lamb spray .
