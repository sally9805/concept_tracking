The leader fled , his subordinates surrendered , and one day last week rebel Biafra ceased to exist . A war was over . The larger significance of that final fact is examined in THE WORLD section , but for America the events in remote Nigeria seemed to possess an unlikely decisiveness . Not since World War II has the U.S. known a war or insurrection that truly , clearly , came to an end-the capitulation signed , the sword surrendered . Not in wars fought : Korea and Viet Nam . Not in conflicts passionately witnessed : Cuba , Hungary , the Middle East , Kashmir . If the Nigerians can resurrect the validity of reconciliation and make a peace that is not war by another name , they may restore an almost forgotten concept to the U.S. arsenal of expectations .
