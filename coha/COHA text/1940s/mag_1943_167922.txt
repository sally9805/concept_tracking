Canada 's wartime Prices and Trade Board decreed two simplifications for Canada 's tables last week : 1 ) it rationed butter to half a pound per week per person ; 2 ) it ordered porcelain manufacturers to abandon fripperies , especially cup handles . <P> The mortality rate in cups is higher than in other dishes , and Canadian housewives can no longer replace their cup losses with imports from England , Japan and Central Europe . Explained a Board member : " Nine million cups without handles can be turned out by the same plant capacity that produces three million with handles . . . . The handles have to be put on by hand .
