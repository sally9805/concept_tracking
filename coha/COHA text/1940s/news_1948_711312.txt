txt The record breaking spring dry spell and above normal temperatures have caused the city to put Chicago 's summer water schedule into effect nearly a month early . W. W. DeBerard , city engineer , disclosed yesterday . Pressure increases of from 4 to 10 pounds above the 20 to 57 pounds winter pressure at all pumping stations began yesterday , to continue thru September . The summer schedule was put into effect June 27 last year , and on June 24 in 1946 : Public Works . Commissioner Hewitt said sufficient water will be available for every one if city ordinances are observed . Sprinkling ' may be done only from 5 to 7 a.m. and from 8:30 to 10:30 p.m. , and the opening of fire hydrants at any time is forbidden . Suburbs using Chicago water were warned to fill reservoirs only during the night . Letters containing an outline of precautions to be taken in the use of water during warm weather were to be sent to big industrial and institutional users .           @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @
