txt Universal Citizenship In the United States and the Englishspeaking nations of the British Commonwealth the individual and his rights are the basis of the system of organized freedom . The future peace and prosperity of the world would be well served by increased unity among the English-speaking nations . A. practical step could be taken toward that end without any change in the formal legal relations of the various nations . Let the laws of the United States and the nations of the British Commonwealth be altered to provide that the citizens of each nation shall have all the rights of a citizen of any other nation of the group in the jurisdiction of which he may find himself legally present as a visitor or resident . o HENRY BRECKINRIDGEL New York , May 5 , 1943.
