Last week the man who has been fighting the Japanese longer than any other world leader gave his answers to some of the war 's persistent questions . In Chungking , at his first press conference since 1941 ( see FOREIGN NEWS ) , Generalissimo Chiang Kai-shek gave this estimate of the situation : <P> An Allied landing on the China coast is needed-the sooner the better . But when the Allies have successfully invaded Japan itself , then Chinese troops should be able to deal with any Japanese forces left on the Asiatic mainland . Japanese surrender is unlikely , and the process of battering Japan into final , total defeat will probably take more than a year and a half , thus running on into 1947.
