The alarm bells of history clanged last week for more than Tokyo . While the air attack on the Empire 's capital was going on , the impatient Americans in Luzon were rushing back onto Bataan and Corregidor . Other Americans swarmed ashore on Iwo Jima-an island as menacingly close to Japan 's heart as Bermuda is to New York . The war-worn Chinese rallied to take back a section of the hard-won Canton-Hankow railway line . A jungle-trekking British force popped up to menace the conquered oil-field district of Burma . There were more fires than fire brigades , around the Empire ; and the alarm bells still clanged.
