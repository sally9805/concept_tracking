Baseball 's bigwigs put up a bold front at their National-American League meeting last week in Manhattan . Bravely they announced plans for an April opening , drafted a new major-league agreement by which Judge Landis ' successor would rule " strictly by the book " ( i.e. , less arbitrarily ) . Left undone was the one big job : election of a new commissioner . <P> Meanwhile , as it must to most public issues , the Gallup poll came to baseball . <P> Asked whether professional baseball should be stopped for the duration , the public gave the game an amber light : 46% said no , 41% yes , 13% undecided .
