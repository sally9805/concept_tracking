President Avila Camacho announced last week that he was about to reorganize the Mexican Army . The plan would involve retiring some 500 generals . <P> Mexico 's generals , spawned by the Madero Revolution of 1911 , which sent longtime dictator Porfirio Daz scurrying to Paris , had multiplied with each succeeding revolt . Now the Mexican Army has so many generals that no one is able to count them accurately . Said a cynical Mexican official : " It is easier to count the soldiers . " <P> The removal of the 500-odd generals from active service was not expected to cause either tears or gunplay . The generals would continue to keep their titles-and their salaries .
