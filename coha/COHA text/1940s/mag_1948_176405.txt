In London , where a busy summer had already spawned half a dozen hits , the new season began briskly with what looked like another success by prolific Terence Rattigan ( The Winslow Boy , O Mistress Mine ) . Called Playbill , it was a program of two one-acters : The Browning Version , a study of an embittered schoolmaster , and A Harlequinade , which pokes fun at highbrow theater . Rattigan , whose annual royalties pile up to about $100,000 , also has a new drama about Alexander the Great scheduled for a winter opening , and is working on a comedy .
