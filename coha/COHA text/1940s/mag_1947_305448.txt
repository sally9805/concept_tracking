The shortage of steel caught up with the automakers last week . Some 50,000 autoworkers were laid off . Chrysler , Dodge , and Plymouth final assembly lines clanked to a stop ; General Motors closed two body plants ; and Briggs Manufacturing Co. shut six body plants . Most plants which still operated slashed production schedules . <P> This week auto production is expected to drop to around 50,000 , from the 70,000-plus average this spring . With luck , the painful climb to volume production could start again in ten days . But automakers , who had pushed production in the first three months of 1947 to a postwar high of 1,144,526 units , doubted that they would do as well in the second quarter .
