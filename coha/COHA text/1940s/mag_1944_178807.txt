Following the 1943 boom and the tax-inspired buying of last spring , sales of fur coats are off sharply all over the U.S. , currently running about 15% below 1943 . Response to the annual August fur promotions was sluggish ; and in all parts of the country , volume continued to lag in September and October . <P> Retailers hope that the warm autumn weather is to blame . But when cold weather strikes , the fur industry fears that its suspicion may be confirmed : that U.S. women are beginning to clutch their money a little tighter-that the boom is over .
