txt British proposals for an international authority to regulate post-war air traffic will be submitted at the International Aviation conference opening In Chicago Nov. 1 , Mid are expected to meet opposition from American delegates In favor of free competition . Plans for the conference were announced in Washington Sept. 11 . The state department has not announced the official list of American delegates . They will be chosen by " different bureaus " of the department , and will represent diplomatic as well as air line interests , a state department spokesman said . In a white paper this week the British government stated it wants an international air convention granting innocent passage permission for foreign civil airplanes to fly over another country and international traffic rights and eliminating subsidies . The paper proposed an international air authority with an operational executive subsidiary , regional panels , and technical subcommissions that would fix quotas for international air traffic , prescribe rates , and establish technical standards .
