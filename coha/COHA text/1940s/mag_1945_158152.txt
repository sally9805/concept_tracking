To the very end of the bitter campaign for Leyte , the Japs kept at their old tricks . The jig was up , but still some of them filtered into a regimental command post of the 32nd Division on a foully dark night . Their helmets were daubed with phosphorous paint for identification . But in close-quarter brawls , many helmets were knocked off . <P> Pint-sized , scrappy Lieut . James Deloach peered through the forest gloom at two men locked in a murderous struggle . He saw that one had a wire looped around the other 's neck , and that the man being strangled was a Jap . When the job was done , he nudged the killer and mumbled approvingly . The killer answered in Japanese . Deloach shot him .
