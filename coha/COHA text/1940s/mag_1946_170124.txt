The hand that rocks the cradle in the U.S. is getting younger . In its current Bulletin , the Population Reference Bureau of Washington predicts that virtually all U.S. babies may eventually be born to women under 35 . <P> In an earlier day , many a mother had babies after 40 ; today the birth rate in that age group is only half what it was in 1918 . The birth rate for teen-aged women ( 15 to 19 ) has increased one-third , while that for the 35 to 39 group has decreased one-third . <P> The Bulletin finds this trend good : it helps reduce infant and maternal mortality , gives women " a greater opportunity to become active in the general affairs of the nation .
