With total passenger miles running 54% over a year ago and many a train full up , the southern railroads-which pioneered travel-building lower fares-decided they might as well charge as much as other U.S. carriers . Last week they got ICC permission to boost their fares from 1.65 a mile to 2.2 , the national rate . <P> If it does not drive passengers away , the extra .55 will bring the roads well over $10,000,000 . The bankrupt Seaboard Air Line , for example , hopes to collect an added $1,250,000 a year , about equal to its net income in 1941.
