Everyone knew that there had been a bumper crop of wartime and postwar babies in the U.S. , but no one seemed to be doing anything about their future schooling . Last week Oscar R. Ewing , head of the Federal Security Agency , warned Congress that the babies , grown to moppet 's estate , were about to swamp the U.S. school system with 1,000,000 extra schoolkids a year : what the country direly needs , said Oscar Ewing , is some 30,000 extra classrooms . Ewing 's estimate of the minimum needed for new school construction in the next ten years : between $8 billion and $10 billion .
