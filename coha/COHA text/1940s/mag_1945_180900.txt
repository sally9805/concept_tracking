During New York 's 17-day newspaper strike Fact Finders Associates , Inc. sampled nonreading readers ' reactions . Last week the results were released : <P> After three days without papers , 76.6% found that the radio did not " completely fulfill " their news needs . After ten days 89.2% said so . ( Most missed were : 1 ) general news , 2 ) editorials , 3 ) columns , 4 ) comics . ) <P> As women got used to doing without advertisements , interest in them declined from 79.1% to 76.1% . <P> After ten days 58.7% missed their papers more than at the beginning of the strike , 29.1% felt as they always had , 12.2% were getting used to it and missed them less .
