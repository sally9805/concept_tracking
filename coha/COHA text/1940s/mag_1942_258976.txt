To Washington , armed with two Bureau of Standards thermometers and a deep suspicion of how well the Government obeyed its own heat-rationing orders , went Baltimore Sun Reporter Carroll E. Williams . <P> Back to his Baltimore city room went Reporter Williams to write a front-page expose : he had found the average Government building temperature to be 76 degrees . Unreported was an incidental finding : While Reporter Williams wrote his story , his thermometers showed that the temperature at his own desk was 77.
