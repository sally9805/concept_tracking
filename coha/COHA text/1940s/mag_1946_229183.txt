Lady Cripps . head of the British United Aid to China Fund , reached Nanking with her young daughter last week , bringing British good will , good works and goods , including a 24-piece dessert service for Madame Chiang Kaishek , and a 40-piece porcelain tea service for Madame Sun Yatsen . The Chinese responded with an enthusiastic welcome , including a poem in her honor by Ambassador to Britain Cheng Tien-hsi : <P> Millions of Hangs and Wangs with open arms <P> Albion 's maiden envoy will embrace , <P> Whose rare benevolence and natural charms <P> Profoundly touch the heart of an ancient race .
