I wish to express my appreciation of the very just and complimentary terms in which you comment in this morning 's TIMES on the collection of landscapes by Mr. Charles Melville Dewey now at the Macbeth Gallery . The beauty of these paintings . subtly and poetically ' wrought out , is evident to the most casual observer . They deserve to be Seen and studied by all lovers of American art , and particularly by the authorities of the art museums of the country , whose chief function should be to secure examples of the work of our best artists . Your critic has done a service to the community as Well As to the cause of American painting by this sympathetic article . It rightly calla Mr. Dewey one of the most important and original masters of our American landscape school . "
