President Bush last week created the Northwestern Hawaiian Islands Marine National Monument , the largest marine preserve in the world . It is 100 times as big as Yosemite and protects species like the Hawaiian monk seal , right , which should thank Laura Bush . She invited Jean-Michel Cousteau , Jacques 's son , to the White House in April to screen a film on the islands . White House Council on Environmental Quality chairman James Connaughton says the President and Cousteau then talked ocean policy over supper ( no fish ) and into the night . Two months later  --  it usually takes two years  --  Bush made his proclamation , adding a surprising green stripe to his legacy .
