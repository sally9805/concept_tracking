At first glance , Denver and Baghdad do n't have much in common , so the White House 's recent announcement that the two will be sister cities came as a surprise . Denver city officials were the most surprised of all . Encouraged by a Denver reservist serving in Baghdad , the partnership is still being negotiated with a Denver-area council of local governments . City Hall was left in the dark . Given the city 's $33 million budget shortfall , some residents balked at a White House suggestion that Denver will provide " humanitarian assistance " to its new sibling . But the two cities may yet find common ground : they both have pothole problems . <P>  --  By Rita Healy
