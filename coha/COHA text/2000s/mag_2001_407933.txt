The French love their olives , as evidenced by the daily ritual of eating tapenade . Olives are an important source of healthy monounsaturated fatty acids , which are important to the structure of the cell membrane ; they also shield cells from the mutations of genes . <p> Extra virgin olive oil . Extra virgin is the highest grade of olive oil . Produced from the first cold pressing of the olives , it is extracted without heat and has a very low acid content . Extra virgin is best for cold foods , vinaigrettes , and as a finishing oil . The flavor varies depending on where and how the olives are grown . @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ stores where you can try different brands to see which flavors you prefer . Olive oil does not improve with age , so use it within 1 to 2 years . <p> Lightly swirl the top of soups , stews , and casseroles with a small amount of extra virgin olive oil to bring out the flavor of the food . This is particularly good for low-fat dishes , because it gives the impression that there 's more fat in the dish .
