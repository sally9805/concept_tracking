Sidebar Rising Appreciation Sidebar Flour is the body of bread , yeast its soul . Yeast is alive and " breathes " out carbon dioxide , which makes dough rise . In fact , the term proof is derived from a baker 's need to confirm that the yeast is indeed alive so that the rising process works . Yeast is naturally present in the air ; starters , made with flour and water , will attract " wild " east spores and Sidebar develop into a mixture that will live as long as it is " fed " more flour and water . Here are some tips for working with yeast and starters . Three types of yeast are widely available commercially : instant active dry yeast , which does not need dissolving prior to incorporating into dry ingredients ( we call for this in our recipes ) ; active dry yeast , which is dissolved in warm water and then added to flour ; and cake or compressed yeast , which is a moist brick of yeast that is mashed and dissolved in water . These three types of yeast can be used interchangeably . Store dry yeast in your refrigerator up to 1 year or until the label expiration date and keep cake yeast , which is very perishable , in your refrigerator for no longer than 2 weeks . Yeast thrives at about 80'F and will die if exposed to too high a temperature ( above 137oF ) and lie dormant if temperatures are too low ( below 60'F ) .
