The birth of the nation is not an altogether blessed event in this canonically loose novel about the Revolutionary War . Patriotism masks hypocrisy and greed . The Founding Fathers cloak private agendas and petty motives in lofty ideals . After decades of antihero worship and historical revision , are there still readers who can be jolted or amused by caricatures of national legends behaving like lesser mortals ? Yet the author seems to have had a chortling good time burlesquing the past in a style that swings between Henry Fielding and Mel Brooks . <P>  --  By R.Z. Sheppard
