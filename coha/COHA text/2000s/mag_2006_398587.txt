Section : AMERICA 'S LARGEST PRIVATE COMPANIES The rush of going-private transactions is fattening our catalog of private American business <p> What 's driving the mad rush to go private ? It could be the Sarbanes-Oxley crackdown on public companies , or the availability of cheap credit in the junk bond market , or the tax shield that comes from replacing equity with debt . Whatever the impetus , the last 12 months have seen 85 going-private transactions via a private acquirer or investor group , with a combined price tag of $89 billion , according to Thomson Financial . Alongside such anciently private outfits as Cargill and Advance Publications our roster of private American business includes names that would be familiar on Wall Street , like Hertz . Other notable rookies : sporting goods retailer Sports Authority , department store chain ShopKo and Travelport , which owns travel site Orbitz . <p> The 394 companies on this list all have at least $1 billion in annual revenue .
