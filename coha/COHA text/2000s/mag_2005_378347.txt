Warning , Detroit : The Asian car companies in your rearview mirror may be closer than they appear . Whereas GM , Ford and DaimlerChrysler posted a record-low monthly U.S. market share of 56.3% in April , Japan 's Big Three ( Toyota , Honda and Nissan ) led Asia 's automakers to a record 37.5% share , according to market-research firm Autodata Corp . " The Japanese are moving into new segments like hybrids and small SUVs , while the Americans are struggling to update their aging product lines , " says Nikko Citigroup analyst Andrew Phillips . At the head of the pack : Nissan , with a 32% gain in sales compared with April 2004.  --  By Jim Frederick/Tokyo
