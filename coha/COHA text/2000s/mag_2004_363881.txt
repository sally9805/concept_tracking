Doctors have long thought the placebo effect is all in the head , and it turns out they may be right . Using functional magnetic resonance imaging , which captures differences in blood flow in the brain , researchers have produced the strongest evidence yet of how the placebo effect works . In a study conducted at the Ann Arbor Veterans Affairs Health System , the University of Michigan and Princeton , the subjects were given harmless but frequently painful electric shocks and then provided with what they were told was a pain-relieving cream . After the bogus cream was applied , nerve activity in the brains of the volunteers changed . The prefrontal cortex , involved in easing pain , became more active , while regions involved in sensing pain quieted down . When it comes to feeling less pain , it seems , you got ta believe . <P>  --  By Alice Park
