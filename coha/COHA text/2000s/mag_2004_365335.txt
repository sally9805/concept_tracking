The Food and Drug Administration announced last week that a new analysis of clinical data backs the idea that some antidepressant drugs increase the risk of suicide in teens . Debate over the issue , which has been simmering for more than a year , is complicated by the fact that depression alone makes suicide more likely and that the risks vary widely among different drugs . ( Prozac , for example , seems to cause fewer problems than other drugs . ) A decision by the FDA on new warning labels will probably come in September . Meanwhile , a study of 400 youths , in the Journal of the American Medical Association , concluded that Prozac works best when combined with cognitive-behavior therapy .
