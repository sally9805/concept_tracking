Recent public-opinion surveys put the pharmaceutical industry in the unpleasant company of cigarette makers and oil giants , which may help explain why U.S. drug companies have been so eager to polish their image . Pfizer , maker of such blockbusters as Lipitor , Viagra and Zoloft , announced last week that it will provide discounted drugs to uninsured Americans , regardless of age or income . Average savings for families making less than $45,000 could be nearly 40% . Pfizer is also expanding a program that provides free medicine to families making less than $31,000 . With politicians under pressure to lift rules preventing Americans from importing cheaper drugs , do n't be surprised if other drugmakers follow Pfizer 's lead .
