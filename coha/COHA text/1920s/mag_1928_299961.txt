In England , statisticians produced three items of interest last week . <P> 1 ) Births increased . There were 3,142 more in April , May and June than in the preceding three months . <P> 2 ) Drunks decreased . Convictions for drunkenness declined by 1,960 , nearly 3% less than the 1926 record . The year 1927 was the soberest year since 1919 . <P> 3 ) Railroads lost . Sir Felix Pole , general manager of the Great Western Railway Co. , said that due to stagnation of industry , wage burdens imposed by the Government , and competition from busses , British railroads were losing about $5,000,000 each and every month .
