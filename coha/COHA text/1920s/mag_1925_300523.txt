The last king fell ; the last knight rode out in a desperate and vain sortie , defying the white death that comes in the end to the gallantest chevalier and the most stubborn chessman . The gods who had directed the battles-chessplayers , the most famous in the world-put on their neat traveling clothes and left Moscow . The International Tournament , which had endured for six weeks , was over . The winner ? There was no excitement about that . E. Bogoljubow , modest Russian , clinched first prize days before the end . Statuvolent Dr. Emanuel Lasker was second , as had been expected ; Jos R. Capablanca ( TIME , Dec. 7 ) wriggled into third place .
