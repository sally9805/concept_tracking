The passion of motor makers to improve their product , and do it in a hurry too , has created in Detroit deep interest in an lleged " mystery car , " which is said to be revolutionary in design and about to be produced by a Detroit motor concern . <P> The consensus of opinion indicates that the new motor will have a single sleeve-valve engine . This engine was perfected in Scotland , and has been widely used in the " Argyll " car abroad . Louis Chevrolet , automobile builder , has recently been trying it out with a view to adapting it for U. S. use . <P> The " Argyll " is said to require only 28 moving parts . Its engine consists of a single sleeve-valve of cylindrical form , having ports at the top and a single universal driving connection at the bottom .
