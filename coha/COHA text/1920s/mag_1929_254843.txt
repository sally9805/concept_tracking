A dull red coat of preservative paint is the usual lot of structural steel , a dull red which is promptly censored by an overcoating of black . But from now on , the structural steel for all skyscrapers whose frames are by the Hay Foundry &; Iron Works of New York will shine yellow in the glare of the sun . The first of them will be the Louis Adler Building , now arising on Seventh Avenue at 37th St. , Manhattan .
