Wearing a choker collar that looked higher than ever , Kenesaw Mountain Landis sat down at his desk in Chicago and stared solemnly and petulantly at the 50 reporters who rose to say good morning to him . Without a word he handed to each a typewritten statement of 2,000 words-his decision in the baseball scandal of having given or taken bribes in 1917 . The statement declared the players innocent . The " gift " from the White Sox to Boston in 1917 was an impropriety . It was not , said the statement , a crime . The Judge himself said nothing . With a twinkle in his eye he took his coat and hat off a hook , and went back to his hotel .
