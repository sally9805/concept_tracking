The Standard Oil Co. ordered , and the Stout Metal Airplane Co. of Michigan promised delivery on Aug. 10 of a " flying house . " <P> The house is to be built in an ordinary fuselage 45 ft. long , supported on the ground by an immense undercarriage fitted with 44 by 10 in. tires , supported in the air by wings spanning 60 ft . There will be cabin accommodations ( including berths ) for six passengers , pilot , mechanic , cook . Features are electric lighting , heating from the engine exhausts , and electric stove and refrigerator system in the cook 's galley , typewriter , writing desk .
