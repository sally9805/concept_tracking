Passenger traffic on the Southern Railway has declined 30% in the last five years , motor travel of course being the competitive influence . But while the auto was reducing passenger income it was increasing freight income . Fairfax Harrison , Southern president , estimated that 15% of Southern 's 1928 freight traffic came from the automotive industry . Since Southern 's 1928 passenger revenue was $24,000,000 , of which 30% would be $7,200,000 ; and its freight revenue was $108,000,000 , of which 15% is $16,200,000 , the horseless carriage on the whole did not do so badly by the iron horse .
