In Caldwell , N. J. , the members of the First Presbyterian Church met to decide what to do with their benevolence budget of $5,500 . By a vote that was three less than unanimous , they decided to discontinue their annual contribution of $250 to the New Jersey Anti-Saloon League . " The League , " they said , " is no longer a charitable organization . . . . " <P> Commented the New York Herald Tribune : " A wise decision . . . . Many thoughtful ministers and laymen have expressed the belief of late that in supporting the dry and other sumptuary causes , the churches have compromised their moral appeal ; that if one would persuade the spirit he should not at the same time threaten the body . . . . "
