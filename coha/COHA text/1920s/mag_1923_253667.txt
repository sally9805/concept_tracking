The Commoner , founded by William Jennings Bryan 22 years ago , has suspended publication . Since the Great Commoner took up Chautauqua , the Cabinet and Florida , the circulation has steadily shrunk until the paper was scarcely read outside of Nebraska . It dwelt too much upon the past and had ceased to be the clarion call of political battle . Time was when every Democratic editor ill the land kept one eye on The Commoner , while the other guided his pen . <P> In recent years , Charles W. Bryan , a brother , was editor , but his election to the Governorship of Nebraska caused him to forsake his task with the April issue .
