There are flippants who think of Ossip Gabrilowitsch as the little conductor with the highest collar in the world . There are others who know him better-as the Russian pianist who came to the U. S. and married Clara Clemens , Mark Twain 's daughter , or as the conductor who went to Detroit and built up an orchestra there . <P> All well-informed on musical matters know him as a musician of outstanding merit whose littlest interpretations are fraught with beauty . So it was last week that Philadelphia greeted him cordially , even as he stood on the throne of so great a god as Leopold Stokowski , away now on his mid-season holiday ; and that Manhattan paid him like honor when he brought the Philadelphia Orchestra there .
