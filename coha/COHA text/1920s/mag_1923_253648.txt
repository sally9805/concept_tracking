The legs , lungs and head of Clarence H. De Mar , of Melrose , Mass. , proved the best marathon combination in the country . De Mar won the 25-mile run at Boston . At present he is our leading distance runner and will probably wear the shield of the United States in the Olympic marathon at Paris in 1924 . His time at Boston was 2 hours 23 minutes , 47 3/5 seconds . In five starts he has won the race three times , run second once and third once . He is 35 years old . <P> Middleaged , gray-haired William J. Kennedy , bricklayer of Port Chester , N. Y. , ran eighth . He was warned by doctors that he had murmur of the heart and should not race . He finished in good shape . " I needed a little exercise , " was his comment .
