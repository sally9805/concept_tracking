For the first time since the Fascisti came into power in 1922 , an important strike was declared in Italy . <P> About 100,000 Fascist engineering-trades workers in the province of Lombardy walked out over a pay question . The Government , which is usually quick to act in suppressing strikes , took no action except to prohibit public meetings and a procession in Milan . Much criticism was heard from employers . <P> Some of the main demands were later accepted and the number of strikers dwindled to about 80,000 . It was anticipated that the remaining employers would soon capitulate .
