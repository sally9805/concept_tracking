At University College , London , 400 delegates met for the Triennial Imperial Education Conference . Represented were all the lands of the British Empire . Solemn sessions were held for five days , much was discussed . Chief attention was given to experiment and new development in education-a day to " the newest methods in the training of teachers , " a day to the Dalton System ( mode of secondary education , perfected by Miss Parkhurst of Dalton , Mass . ) . Eminent literary men delivered addresses on " English as a Bond of Empire . " At one of the sessions , Alfred Noyes , poet and former lecturer at Princeton University , presided .
