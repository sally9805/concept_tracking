txt " The war left the miners with an exaggerated Idea of their own power . Ily calling out the safety men and breaking down pits we lost all public sympathy . Today the miners are paying the price of their folly in misery and idleness . We ruined our prospects for a good settlement by sticking to the wages pool which the nation feared was a sinister attempt at nationalization . " Thus Herbert Smith . the president of the miners ' federation , castigated the miners at their annual conference at Llandudno today . There , were 150 delegates present and the air was described as unusually subdued . Mr. Smith did not minimize or excuse the defeat . Frank Ilodges , the general secretary of the miners , was equally frank in admitting that it was the pool demand and the withdrawal of the safety men that beat them .
