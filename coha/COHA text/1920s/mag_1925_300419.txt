In the harbor at San Pedro , the Seattle , flagship of the U. S. fleet , lay at anchor . On her deck Admiral Samuel S. Robison , *sturdy stocky , determined , dark-complexioned with iron-grey hair and a close-clipped mustache , faced Admiral Robert E. Coontz , for two years Commander-in-Chief of the U. S. fleet . Everyone wore his very best bib and uniform , immaculate , ceremonial . There were a few stiff movements , an exchange of salutes and Samuel S. Robison became Commander-in-Chief of the U. S. fleet <P> <P> *Not to be confused with Captain J. K. Robison . ( See Above. )
