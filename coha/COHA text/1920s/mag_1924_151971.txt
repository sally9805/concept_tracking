So many and so varied were last week 's despatches relative to the Chinese civil war ( TIME , Sept. 8 et seq. ) that they passed comprehension . Bloody fighting in the North and bloody fighting around Shanghai took place without decisive result . Losses were heavy . A rumor persisted that many of the Peking Government 's troops had gone over to the enemy . One report stated that Super-Tuchun Chang of Manchuria was advancing on Peking ; the rest that he was retiring on Mukden , his capital . The only report that all were agreed upon was one describing the opening by Chang of his own private Foreign Office .
