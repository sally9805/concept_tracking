" Peretous , " the nom de plume of a writer of the Moscow Izvestiya , gave the lie to rumors of the impending entry of Bolshevikland into the League of Nations : <P> " Soviet Russia , " he scoffed , " could never agree with the fundamental aim of the League to consolidate the present status of State borders ; and we would never permit the League to compel us to submit to arbitration the vital interests of the Soviet Union , as there can not exist impartial arbitration between the Soviet economic system and capitalistic society . " <P> Reminding his readers that the Soviet Government has its own world program , the writer continued : <P> " Time works for us ; and Russia can afford , without alarm , to watch calmly the League of Nations , which she fears is a Colossus with feet of clay . "
