In reply to Senator de Monzie who wished to know if France could not now resume commercial relations with Sovietland , Premier Poincar said that France 's attitude to Russia was the same as that of the U.S. <P> He asseverated that he would not jeopardize the interests of French citizens for the sake of winning advantages which some firms might derive from trading with Russia . <P> He had , he said , only asked Russia to recognize the usual principles regulating the relations between nations , and until she agreed to pay her debts and indemnify French investors for their losses , he could not change his attitude .
