A luncheon and clinic at the Henry Ford Hospital was arranged for all members of the Detroit Academy of Surgery . Invitations were issued . A very few members accepted . The arrangements were cancelled . <P> The objections of the Detroit surgeons to the Ford institution were : <P> " Alleged application of factory methods to the handling of patients . . . " Elimination of the personal relationship between physician and patient . . . . <P> " Refusal to take charity patients . . . . <P> " Uniform fees , said to impose burdens upon the poor and to render difficult the collection by private physicians or surgeons , of adequate fees from the rich . . . . <P> " Refusal of the institution to permit physicians or surgeons , other than staff members , to treat patients . . . . <P> " Alleged unethical advertising by pamphlets .
