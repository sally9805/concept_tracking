THE DARK DAYS OF 1864 . IN the memory of men who lived in Washington during the months of July and August , 1864 , those days will appear to be the darkest of the many dark days through which passed the friends and lovers of the Federal Union . The earlier years of the war , it is true , had been full of grief despondency , and even agony ; but the darkness that settled upon us in the summer of 1864 was the more difficult to be endured because ofits unexpectedness . The hopes so buoyantly entertained by our people when Grant opened his campaign in Virginia had been dashed . No joyful tidings came from the army now ; a deadly calm prevailed where had so lately resounded the shouts of victory . In every department of the Government there was a manifest feeling of discouragement . In the field of national politics confusion reigned .
