A brand-new , high-tech business blasted off in the U.S. last week : satellite launching . Martin Marietta , which manufactures Titan-class rockets for the Air Force , signed an agreement with Federal Express to send aloft its ExpressStar communications satellite in 1989 . President Reagan had opened the way for the new industry last month , when he an- nounced that NASA will drastically reduce the number of commercial cargoes carried aboard the space shuttle . <P> Martin Marietta hopes for a big share of the estimated $1 billion annual launching market that the European Space Agency has virtually had to itself since January 's shuttle tragedy . But others are eyeing the pie . Among them : General Dynamics , which has built Atlas-class rockets , and McDonnell Douglas , the maker of Delta boosters .
