Think of the classic convertible cars : long , sleek , sporty , maybe even a bit impractical . All in all , not the kind of vehicle that would lend itself to a gun rack behind the driver 's seat or a load of cargo bouncing around in the back . Think again : Chrysler , the No. 3 U.S. automaker , plans to introduce in early 1989 a Dodge Dakota pickup truck with a removable , manually operated vinyl top . <P> At about $13,000 , the model would be one of the lowest-priced convertibles in the American market . Ford and Toyota are said to be mulling their own moves into convertible pickups.
