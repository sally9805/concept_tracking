Are American youngsters as physically fit as their Soviet counterparts ? No , says the President 's Council on Physical Fitness and Sports . To prove it , the council has arranged for 20,000 Americans to take a fitness test given yearly in Soviet schools ; in exchange , the U.S.S.R. Committee on Physical Culture and Sport will try the council 's test on an equal number of Soviet youth . The experiment started earlier this month in El Segundo , Calif. , where 250 students ages seven to 17 sweated through the Soviet test . <P> Fitness Council Chairman George Allen expects that Americans will look bad compared with Soviet students , who exercise an hour daily . He hopes the test will spur the U.S. into putting physical education " back in the school + systems , an hour a day , five days a week , kindergarten through twelfth . "
