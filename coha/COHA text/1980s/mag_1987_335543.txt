Just six inches from tip to tail , the tiny brown birds made their home in a ten-mile coastal stretch of marsh near Titusville , Fla . When development from nearby Cape Canaveral began to encroach , they stubbornly refused to move , and their numbers declined relentlessly . Last week the last known Dusky Seaside Sparrow expired : Orange Band , a twelve-year-old male , was found dead in its + cage . <P> Scientists tried to save the bird from extinction by crossbreeding it with a hardier sparrow . But Orange Band died before they could complete the job , leaving five hybrid Dusky Seasides  --  one of them seven-eighths pure .
