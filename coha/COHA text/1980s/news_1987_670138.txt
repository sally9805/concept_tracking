The decline in the dollar didn ' t stop Tokyo stock prices from inching up to another record , but it did knock down prices in London and Frankfurt . Tokyo prices were up sharply early in the session on the heels of modest gains on Wall Street Wednesday . In addition , traders were analyzing the effect of the currency meeting in Washington among the major industrialized nations , where the group reaffirmed the stabilization accord reached in February . But then a flurry of rumors that Finance Ministry officials asked Japan ' s major securities houses to cool overheated share prices and curb excess speculation sparked a sudden wave of profit-taking , traders said . The Nikkei stock average lost 1. 5% of its value in the last hour of afternoon trading , as investors feared the rumors would spark a sudden correction and wanted to limit their exposure . The Nikkei average closed up 9. 21 points at 22922. 20 . It was the third consecutive record close , but volume contracted sharply to 1. 4 billion shares from two billion shares Wednesday .
