Daylight saving time , first suggested by Benjamin Franklin in 1784 as a means of cutting down on candle consumption , is a proven conservator of another of Franklin 's tinkering projects , namely electricity . But the system also forces some early risers in the springtime to start their day in the dark . The dilemma : When best to make the twice-yearly time change ? <P> Last week Congress apparently decided the U.S. should " spring forward " three weeks earlier than it does now . The Senate passed a bill setting the first Sunday in April as the opening of daylight time , beginning next year . ) Observers expect that the bill will be reconciled with a similar measure passed by the House , which would also add a week at the end of daylight time before the annual " fall back .
