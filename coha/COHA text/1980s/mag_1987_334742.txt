It was one of the more transparent congressional maneuvers in recent memory : lawmakers last February voted against boosting their annual salaries from $77,400 to $89,500  --  one day after a presidential order establishing the pay hike had gone into effect . The move allowed Congressmen to tell constituents they opposed the increase even as they pocketed the extra cash . <P> But as the first installment of the raise appeared in congressional paychecks last week , 57 House members and 13 Senators said no to the $12,100 pay hike . Most of the refuseniks donated the money to charity or returned it to the U.S. Treasury . The honorable gesture shocked one of Senator Alan Simpson 's most loyal supporters . Says the Wyoming Republican : " When I told my wife I gave the money back , she said , ' You did what ? ' "
