Publishers from Hearst to Hefner have used the maxim " Sex sells " to highly profitable advantage . Businessmen in southern China were following that capitalist road until last week , when Communist Party officials in Guangxi province shut down 39 popular magazines and journals . It was the biggest press crackdown since the campaign against " bourgeois liberalism " was launched four months ago . <P> Most of the widely read magazines focused on sex , adventure or kung fu . Some claimed to be serious literary or art journals , including a scholarly legal review that carried articles like " Why the Breast of a Woman Was Tattooed . " While some Chinese writers agree that the more vulgar periodicals should be weeded out , they are concerned that the crackdown may herald tougher censorship .
