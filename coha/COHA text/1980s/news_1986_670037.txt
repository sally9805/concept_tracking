DETROIT  --  American Motors Corp. will temporarily close its Kenosha , Wis. , car assembly plant next week to help reduce unsold inventories of the Renault Alliance and Encore subcompacts built there . Sales of the cars since January have fallen more than 39% below the year-ago level and the Kenosha plant has worked only seven weeks in 1986 . As previously reported , General Motors Corp. is eliminating the second shift at its Arlington , Texas , and Bowling Green , Ky. , car assembly plants starting next week , indefinitely idling about 2 , 000 workers . The action , like the temporary shutdown at Kenosha , is designed to cut inventories of slow-selling cars : the mid-sized Chevrolet Monte Carlo , the Oldsmobile Supreme and the Chevrolet Corvette . Two other GM plants and the car-assembly line at Nissan Motor Co. ' s Tennessee plant remain down next week for model changeover . Chrysler Corp. ' s Sterling Heights , Mich. , plant closes next week for a five-week retooling to prepare for production of the new Plymouth Sundance and Dodge Shadow models .
