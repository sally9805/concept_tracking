With the Seoul Olympics only two months away , the nightmarish specter of a North Korean terrorist attack at the Games haunts South Korean officials . Thus South Korean President Roh Tae Woo proposed a dramatic improvement in North- South relations . He called for moves ranging from a resumption of mail service between the two Koreas to the reunion of families divided by the border . <P> Pyongyang last week denounced the offer as " nothing new " and repeated its own demand for a North-South conference on reunification . Seoul , which has condemned such a gathering as " unrealistic " and " impractical , " said it will nonetheless continue to seek increased trade and diplomatic ties with the North and help Pyongyang join international organizations . In a further display of goodwill , South Korea halted daily propaganda broadcasts to the North at least until after the Olympics .
