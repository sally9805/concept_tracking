Every hundred years , some wag remarked as the Statue of Liberty was undergoing its two-year , $66.3 million restoration , a lady needs a face-lifting . Well , maybe it was too much to expect the refurbished copper statue to shine like a newly minted penny at its rededication on July 4 . Even so , why have parts of the statue 's left cheek , left neck and torch arm developed what the New York Daily News last week delicately dubbed a skin problem ? The dark spots , it turns out , are acid stains , caused by pollutants that began eating away at the statue 's protective patina in the 1960s and can not be removed without endangering the delicate copper sheath . But then again , what 100-year-old lady does not have an age spot here and there ?
