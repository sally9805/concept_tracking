For every reform there is a loophole . Under current congressional ethics codes , if an organization pays a politician to give a speech , it is permitted to pay him reasonable travel expenses . As a result , reports the citizens ' lobby Common Cause , all-expense-paid nights away from home have increased 50% among Senators over the past five years and 200% among Congressmen . One example : Minnesota Senate Republican David Durenberger and his two sons , 21 and 23 , were able to wrap a free and entirely legal six-day Caribbean vacation around the Senator 's informal meeting with Puerto Rican health officials and a speech he delivered to Squibb Corp . executives . <P> The trip was given courtesy of the Puerto Rico , U.S.A. Foundation and a lobbying Washington law firm .
