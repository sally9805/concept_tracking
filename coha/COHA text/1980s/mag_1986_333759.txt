As the Yuletide season gets under way , shoppers from Bonn to Boston to Buenos Aires can already hear carols resounding through stores . Some salesclerks , however , may find the ceaseless piped-in tunes too much of a good thing . In Linz , Austria , suffering department-store workers have sought their union 's help . " It 's a clear case of psychoterror , " said Eduard Anger , president of the Union of Employees in Private Industry . Relentless repetition of standards like Jingle Bells can cause headaches and leave listeners dizzy , Anger says . He is asking stores to cut some of the caroling , raising clerks ' hopes for more silence and less Silent Night .
