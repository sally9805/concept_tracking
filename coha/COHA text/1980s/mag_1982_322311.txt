The only authorized antiwar movement in Moscow is the Kremlin-run Soviet Peace Committee , which claims to have 80 million members . Last week eleven young scientists and engineers announced the creation of an independent movement . They read an appeal , already mailed to President Leonid Brezhnev and Soviet newspapers , calling upon the governments of the U.S. and the U.S.S.R. to stop denouncing each other . They asked for an uncensored flow of information between Western and Soviet peace groups . The Kremlin is unlikely to welcome an unfettered peace group , however small and softspoken . But in view of its stentorian approval of peace activists in the West , it may be too embarrassed to do much about the new organization-at least for the time being .
