One would think that with the annual failure of his pennant boasts , New York Yankees Owner George Steinbrenner would find the business of baseball risky enough . Think again . Last week Steinbrenner was at the starting gate of Florida 's Pompano Park Harness Raceway , sitting in a sulky alongside such sporting celebs as Writer George Plimpton and Yankee Hall of Famer Whitey Ford . " When you become a businessman you become stagnant in some ways , " explains Steinbrenner , who owns a trotter and a pacer . " You do n't do as many of the exciting and dangerous things you used to do . It was skydiving or this . " Of course , if Steinbrenner is still thrill seeking after winning the charity race , he can always bring back Billy Martin to manage .
