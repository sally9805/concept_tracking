In the Soviet Union , people have usually kept their views to themselves . Thus it came as a surprise last week when the Soviet daily Izvestia published an interview based on two public-opinion surveys . The polls were the latest example of Soviet Leader Mikhail Gorbachev 's policy of glasnost , or openness . <P> Pollster Vilen Ivanov said he found that Soviet workers feel Gorbachev 's economic reforms have so far meant more work , less growth and lower incomes . " The worker 's job has not yet undergone any radical change in character , organization or pay , " Ivanov told Izvestia . Still , when people were asked their overall view on Gorbachev 's economic policies , 90% declared their full support , and only .6% expressed opposition .
