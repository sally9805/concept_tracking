Little Brain Cell <P> Vacuum tubes are the brain cells of modern technology . Each year , as machines take on more complex jobs , more &; more vacuum tubes are needed . But they are tricky to manufacture : they are usually both bulky and fragile . <P> Last week Bell Telephone Laboratories demonstrated a small , simple device that can do many of the jobs now done by vacuum tubes . Called a " Transistor , " it is a slim metal cylinder about an inch long . Inside are two hair-thin wires whose points press , two-thousandths of an inch apart , on a pinhead of germanium.
