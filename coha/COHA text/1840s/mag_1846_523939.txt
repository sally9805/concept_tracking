The influence of language upon thought , has been a subject of much philosophical speculation ; its importance can hardly be overrated ; and in the present case it deserves careful attention . The vernacular language of deaf mutes , which is in reality used by them everywhere , and generally more or less through life , can not but have an important influence on their mode of thinking .
