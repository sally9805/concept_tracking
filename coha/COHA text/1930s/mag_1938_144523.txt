The two leading politicians in the little village of Ecorse just outside Detroit are William W. Voisine and WT . Newton Hawkins . William Voisine was President of the Village from 1933 to 1936 . In the 1936 election he was re-elected by one vote . Newton Hawkins asked for a recount , in which William Voisine 's margin was increased to ten . Newton Hawkins took it to the Michigan Supreme Court . Last month the Supreme Court Justices found the election a draw , ordered the two politicians to decide the issue by lot . Last week they pulled slips out of a hat . Newton Hawkins got the slip lettered ELECTED . Promptly he promised to start suit for the $2,000 salary William Voisine drew while he was illegally president .
