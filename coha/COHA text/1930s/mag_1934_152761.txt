Four months ago Publisher John Charles Martin , son-in-law of the late Cyrus H. K. Curtis , sold his losing New York Evening Post* to J. David Stern , publisher of the virile Philadelphia Record . Three weeks ago there was a report in Philadelphia that Curtis heirs planned to dispose of the morning Public Ledger , now losing heavily ( TIME , March 26 ) . This week that news was confirmed when the Public Ledger announced that its morning and Sunday editions would be merged at once with the Curtis-Martin Philadelphia morning Inquirer ( circulation : 210,795 ) . The Evening Ledger will continue unchanged . <P> *Last week the Post 's editor , Dr. Ernest Henry Omening , famed liberal , resigned after a row with Publisher Stern .
