txt A sharp adjustment in cattle has recently taken place , with the average run of cattle nearly doubled in price , in the last 60 days . At the turn of the year , in spite of the low price and the stripping of the ranges and farms of surpluses , due to the drought , bankers were reluctant to advance funds to buy cattle . There was no feed , winter frnpended , and if . bad weather forced marketing , there was no place to go . The shortage of corn made fat cattle scarce and packers first ran prices up . Then everyone got in , scrambling for any sort of beef animal . Farmers from Iowa and Nebraska were buyers , bankers became willing lenders . California buyers , who usually secured killing stock from Texas , came to Kansas . A series of country auction markets have sprung up . The one in Dodge City has had unprecedented sales .
