&gt; Martin Thomas Manton , 58 , former senior judge of the U. S. Circuit Court of Appeals in Manhattan ( TIME , June 5 ) , was found guilty by a jury of " selling justice " to rich litigants and others for at least $235,600 . Maximum penalty : two years in jail , $10,000 fine . He was not only the first Federal appellate court judge ever to be convicted , but the first ever to be tried for selling justice . More than 3,000 cases tried before him in 21 years may be reopened . He appealed .
