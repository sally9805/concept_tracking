U. S. physicians are proud of the fact that they donate one million dollars ' worth of free medical services to the needy every day and never refuse to help anyone , whether he can pay or not . Last week Southern physicians gasped as they heard of a bold infringement of this humanitarian code . Five of the eight doctors serving the 3,000 souls of Atmore , Ala. , and five of their colleagues in cotton-growing Escambia County printed an advertisement in the Atmore Advance warning Escambia County 's prospective mothers that henceforth they would demand cash on delivery . The ad : " We wish to take this means of letting the public know that ... all obstetrical ( labor ) cases will be done on a strictly cash basis . "
