Personally prosperous Richard Bedford Bennett , Premier of Canada , officially turned down last week the Dominion 's chance of a lifetime to buy Labrador ( 110,000 square miles ) for the bargain price of $110,000,000 . Premier Sir Richard Anderson Squires of Newfoundland , who offered this bargain to Canada , desperately needs the money to save himself from another thorough pummelling and beating by Newfoundland 's unemployed who demand a larger " dole . " If the U. S. cares to bid for Labrador , now is the time . <P> In Ottawa , Mr. Bennett not only refused to buy Labrador , he even slashed $57,000,000 from the Canadian Government 's estimate of what it must spend during the Canadian fiscal year beginning on April Fool 's Day 1932.
