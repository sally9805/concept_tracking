At some early , secret date , with 152 soldiers aboard and thousands more strung along the route , the first of some 50 armored special trains will begin moving 192,000,000 oz. of government gold worth $6,000,000,000 from New York , Philadelphia and other vaults to the great new fortress-vault at Fort Knox , Ky . Ordinary railroad charge for such a haul would be some $200,000 . Last week it was revealed that the Treasury would take advantage of government mail contracts , send its gold by registered parcel post . At the standard rate of 10 per oz. , the postage bill to a private sender would be $19,200,000 . Generous Postmaster General Farley is expected to charge Secretary Morgenthau only about $100,000.
