When Greece recalled King George II from placid exile in shabby , respectable Brown 's Hotel in London last year , it sucked him into a political vortex of danger and unease so critical that his friends were unfairly suspected of having poisoned his chief antagonist , the late General-Dictator George Kondylis ( TIME , Feb. 10 ) . Last week King George , normally a sedate and cautious citizen , was driving in nervous haste down an Athens street when a street car suddenly lumbered around a corner directly into his path .
