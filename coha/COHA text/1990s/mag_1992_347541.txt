For a few days , it looked as if the on-again-off-again negotiations for a worldwide trade accord , as part of the Uruguay Round of GATT talks , were finally coming to an end . High-level U.S. and E.C . leaders were said to be ever so close to signing an agreement . They were n't close enough . At the last minute the talks foundered on the same sensitive issue that has persistently stymied negotiators : farm subsidies . Unless France reverses its rigid opposition to European concessions , it appears that a trade pact , if one is ever signed , will come too late to bolster George Bush 's election prospects .
