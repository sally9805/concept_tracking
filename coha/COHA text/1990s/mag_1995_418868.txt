KWANZAA , a seven-day celebration that occurs between December 26 and January 1 , is a cultural observance that gives African-Americans an opportunity to renew and reaffirm those values and strengths that lie at the root of our history . Kwanzaa was created in 1966 by Dr. Maulana Karenga , chairman of the Black Studies department at California State University-Long Beach . The word Kwanzaa comes from the Swahili phrase " Matuna Ya Kwanza , " which means " first fruits of the harvest . " There are seven principles ( nguzo saba ) that are celebrated during Kwanzaa , one for each day of the week . On the last day of Kwanzaa , family and friends are invited to attend Karamu , which means " feast . " Everyone who attends the feast brings food to share with the group . If you are hosting or attending Karamu , here are some tasty recipes that reflect some of the cuisines of Black people from around the world . Mkeka. a mat which represents African tradition , is the symbol which all the other symbols rest . Kikombe cha umoja is the communal cup which is a symbol of unity and is used in the ceremony to share the drink . When people drink from this cup , they honor . all the people who lived before them . Kinara is a seven-branched candleholder that 's a symbol for people who are the ancestors of African-Americans . Mishumaa saba are the seven candles ; representing the seven principles of Kwanzaa . There is one black candle , three red candles and three green ones . On each night of Kwanzaa , a new candle is lit . Muhindi is an ear of dry corn for each child in the family . Zawadi , a collection of gifts that parents give to their children , represents rewards for outstanding achievement in applying Kwanzaa values throughout the year .
