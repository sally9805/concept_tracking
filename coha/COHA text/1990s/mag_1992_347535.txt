WANT A PREVIEW OF HOW FIERCELY INSURANCE COMPANIES might battle Bill Clinton 's health-care proposals ? Look at the way national insurers are pouring cash into California to defeat PROPOSITION 166 , which would require all employers to provide health coverage for workers . Fearing that medical costs could billow under the plan , insurance firms with headquarters outside the state have put up more than 80% of the $2.3 million that opponents have raised to defeat it . The companies also fear that Clinton 's proposal for a government-run program for employees not covered at work could lead to federal control of health insurance . Frets one executive about the Clinton plan : " It 's the camel 's nose under the tent . "
