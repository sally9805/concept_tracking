When a band sells 10 million albums its first time around , it ceases to be a band and becomes a franchise . Its objective turns from growth to survival . That 's surely what Millennium 's producers were thinking when they decided to reprise the same old boy-band routines  --  the synthetic arrangements , the pseudo-soul vocals . The sound draws scads of fans , but in the year since the BB 's debut , half a dozen clones have flooded the market . Millennium might have seemed distinctive even , if it had taken a fraction of a chance . <P>  --  By David E. Thigpen
