No town is more emblematic of the South 's segregationist past than Selma , Alabama , where black Baptist clergyman Martin Luther King Jr . led a climactic civil rights march in 1965 . But a racial line has now disappeared in Selma , as delegates from 24 congregations in Alabama 's dominant Southern Baptist faith voted to admit the Freedom Baptist Church as the first black member of the city 's Baptist association . Said newly entering pastor Letha Rumph : " I can see that a revival has begun in Selma . " A white colleague , the Rev. Ron Davis , chimed in : " God wo n't let us fail . "
