Premature babies are often plagued with health problems , so women who start labor too early frequently take a drug called ritodrine to stop their contractions . But a study in the New England Journal of Medicine has found that though ritodrine is the only drug approved for that purpose by the Food and Drug Administration , it is actually close to useless  --  while its side effects , including fluid in the lungs , can in rare cases lead to death . The FDA is pondering its next move .
