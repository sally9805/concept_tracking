The 1980s were difficult years for the civil rights movement , but when it came to entrepreneurship , black Americans made substantial strides . The government said last week that the number of black-owned businesses rose from 308,000 in 1982 to 424,000 in 1987 , an increase of 38% . At the same time , the overall number of U.S. firms grew just 14% . <P> The survey also showed that the average black enterprise had annual revenues of $47,000 during the period , vs. an average of $146,000 for all small and medium-size U.S. companies . Yet government efforts to steer contracts to black firms are under attack from conservatives , who call this a form of discrimination . Says Henry Henderson , an electronics entrepreneur in New Jersey : " It is now more difficult to set aside projects strictly for minority companies . "
