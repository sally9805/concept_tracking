Ashton Robinson talks for a living  --  in infomercials , at seminars and on tapes that establish him as the biggest mouth in the self-help industry . But one day , while tripping out on weed and expired cough syrup , he is visited by a shape-shifting Brazilian midget who inspires him to found his own religion . The guru turns his California pad into a temple and teaches his followers to wear Gap clothing , bungee-jump and practice Tantric sex in preparation for their own " disappearance "  --  nirvana in Robinson 's religion . The novel is a patchwork of clever ideas that never quite settle into a story . It 's a relief when Robinson finally , inevitably , " disappears . " <P>  --  By Nadya Labi
