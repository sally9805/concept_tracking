POLICY DISPUTES . According to insurance-industry executives , publisher Malcolm Forbes took out millions of dollars worth of life insurance with different companies in the years preceding his death . He often said this was an efficient way to pass along money to his heirs , but now at least one of the insurers is balking  --  and investigating some of the information the publisher supplied on his applications . Says Richard Bevilacqua , public relations director for John Hancock : " Forbes had a multimillion-dollar policy that was taken out in the last two years . A claim has been filed , but we have not paid it , pending an investigation . "
