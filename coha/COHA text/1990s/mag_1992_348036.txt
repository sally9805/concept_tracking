FOR YEARS AUDIENCES HAVE CRIED AT THE SIGHT OF the undersized lad bearing a single crutch who rides atop his father 's shoulder . But Charles Dickens never revealed just what is wrong with Tiny Tim Cratchit , whose life is in chronic peril each Christmas . American pediatric neurologist Donald Lewis apparently could n't take it anymore . After examining the literature , literally , on Tim , Dr. Lewis has come up with a professional diagnosis : distal renal tubular acidosis . According to A Christmas Carol , Tiny Tim lives to enjoy Christmases Yet to Come , thanks to a reformed Scrooge and his trio of conscience-raising phantoms . Dr. Lewis ' analysis does n't rule out that happy outcome  --  medics of the time had an effective antacid treatment for Tim 's condition .
