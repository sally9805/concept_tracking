Since women are the nation 's top consumers , advertising has always made them the center of its world . Ads portrayed proper female aspirations : to be a desirable companion , a competent cleaner , a loving mother . The women in ads found fulfillment in the supermarket aisles  --  and in Maidenform bras . But as millions began to venture beyond the home in the 1970s , the images had to change . Madison Avenue 's women developed minds of their own . Consider the female Honda buyer , who thinks like a man . Or Charlie , reaching out to touch someone . Even romance mirrors complex modern reality : the cute young thing in the new Johnnie Walker ad seems to be a divorced mother .
