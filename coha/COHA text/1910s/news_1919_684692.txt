txt Washington  --  The Senate Tuesday passed the Edge bill authorizing organization of foreign banking corporations under thie supervision of the Federal Reserve Board . The bill was amended in many respects . Foreign banking corporations are prohibited from engaging in commerce or dealing in foodstuffs or from interfering ' with freedom of competition in sale of commodities . Another amendment makes shareholders in the corporations liable to double the amount of their stock subscriptions and renders them liable to Federal taxes imposed on other banking corporations . Investments of any banking corporation in any one enterprise are limited to 10% of the bank 's capital and surplus . On motion of Senator Owen , a provision exempting foreign banking corporations from anti-trust laws was sigicken out- Other amendments were adopted to insure American control of all concerns organized under the act .
