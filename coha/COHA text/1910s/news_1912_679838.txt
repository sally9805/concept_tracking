txt MYSTIC DIRECTIONS . The discussion about the " unacquainted ones " touched upon by some of your correspondents seems to suggest this condition : Those who could best work out a plan are the married ones , and they are snug in a sense of security as having " arrived , " and are no longer worrying themselves on the subject . The unmarried are debarred , from a sense of modesty or otherwise , from taking the initiative . In short , those who can do not and those who would can not . The riffraff seem to marry easily , ( no offense , married reader ; the riffraff are reading some other paper than THE TIMES. ) and those who fall to find their affinities ( although they surely exist ) comprise the better classes . It is decidedly " up to " the married folk to prove themselves worthy of ' their good fortune by doing something practical for others .
