txt Although the war orders under negotiation by the General Electric Co. will mean putting operations of the plants up to capacity , no financing will be required by the company . The terms of war contracts with Great Britain provide for such incidentals as requisite working capital , advances being made where necessary . For instance . one . of the large rifle companies which was among the first plants to receive orders from Great Britain last summer , received an advance of $1,000.000 with which to begin the erection of additional plants and install machinery to produce the guns called for . The making of the General Electric the central and directing unit of a group of concerns turning out war materials will not affect the volume of business which the electric company itself will receive . As has been stated before , the business to he taken by General Electric will only he limited by its capacity to handle it .
