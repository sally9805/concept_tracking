txt Cavalrymen 's Canteens . Having spent the Winter in France taking care of the wounded soldiers . I have many times listened to them recount the unutterable agony they suffered schlle lying unattended on the battlefield when they had no water ; and again and again I have heard how a little water given at such time has undoubtedly saved many a man 's reason , if not his life . It is. then , with nothing less than horror that I learn of a regulation requiring our cavalryman to carry his canteen on his saddle . In case of being wounded and falling from his hoeso he loses , perhaps , the one thing that could save for America a true and brave citizen . This same grave error existed In France at the beginning of the present war ; but Its folly was quickly realized and as quickly rectified .
