After bringing Berlin to the boil , Russia last week let the city simmer a while . Soviet armored cars continued to shuttle through the Western sector ; they drew only scowls from the crowds that had stoned them the week before . More than 300 East Berliners turned out for the funeral of Peter Fechter , the 18-year-old bricklayer who had been shot and left to die while trying to cross the Wall . Surest sign of relaxed tensions was the Western alliance 's return to disarray . Despite President Kennedy 's announcement that the Big Four had agreed " in principle " to a foreign ministers ' conference on Berlin , they all seemed to have previous engagements . At week 's end , the Big Four conference was off-like the pressure .
