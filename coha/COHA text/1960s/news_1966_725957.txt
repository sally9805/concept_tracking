txt Masses of stagnant air wrapped the New York metropolitan area in a sooty blanket tonight , pushing the city 's air pollution index past the danger point . The smog , which thickened as night fell and the air became dense with automobile exhaust fume s , presented a general health hazard and a serious threat to persons with respiratory ailments . Austin N. Heller , the city 's commissioner of pollution control , said the danger index reached 60.6 between 8 and 9 p. m. ? five times the normal amount of harmful materials in the air . The index measures the amount of sulphur dioxide and carbon monoxide in the atmosphere . A reading of 50 is considered an indication of dangerous pollution . Heller said he was considering calling a first level alert in the city 's air pollution warning system .
