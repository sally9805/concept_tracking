txt Removal of 2 1/2 million cubic yards of sand from the Indiana dunes to be used to extend the Northwestern university campus by 70 acres will begin today , James Chester . legal advisor for the Bethlehem Steel corporation , said yesterday . The operation is expected to take all summer . Mrs. James Buell , president of the Save the Dunes council , said the move will spark renewed efforts to halt the project . The proposed sand transfer has been the subject of controversy for more than eight months . The Farmers State Bank of Valparaiso , an agent for Bethlehem , is selling the sand to Northwestern for 7 cents a yard . Chester said the first phase of a program to build a deepwater port at the Indiana dunes site is near completion . He said a quarter-mile harbor with a turning basin has been dug .
