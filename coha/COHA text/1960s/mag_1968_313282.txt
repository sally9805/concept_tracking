Political arithmetic five weeks before the Republican Convention seems to indicate that Richard Nixon is assured a first-ballot victory . After Texas Senator John Tower , a favorite son , released 44 delegates to Nixon last week , the former Vice President appeared comfortably past the 667 votes he will need for the nomination . New York 's Governor Nelson Rockefeller had 268 delegate votes , followed by California Governor Ronald Reagan with 161 .
