Germans who patronize shops at the foot of a skyscraper struggle against gatelike winds . It 's a worldwide problem in the architectural heyday of solitary skyscrapers surrounded by lower buildings . In Boston , workers are busy glassing in portions of the Prudential Center mall in an effort to break the wind . British researchers have turned out a study which explains the problem but offers little in the way of solution . The diagrams at right show how a group of buildings of the same relative height tends to slow down the wind .
